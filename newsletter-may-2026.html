Why May Matters More Than You Think

Many business owners treat bookkeeping as a once-a-year task. In reality, the businesses that stay organized after tax season are the ones that:

  • Avoid costly surprises
  • Make better financial decisions
  • Reduce tax-prep fees and stress

Think of May as your financial "fresh start."

Key Tax Tips for May 2026

1. Start planning for your next estimated tax payment

Your next quarterly estimated tax payment is due June 17, 2026. If you're self-employed or earning income outside of a W-2 job, now is the time to:

  • Review your year-to-date income
  • Set aside cash for your payment
  • Adjust your estimates if this year looks different than last

Waiting until June often leads to underpayment penalties or cash-flow stress.

2. Reevaluate your tax strategy

Did your tax bill catch you off guard this year? That's usually a sign that something needs adjusting. In May, you still have time to:

  • Update your withholding (if applicable)
  • Refine your estimated tax calculations
  • Implement proactive tax-planning strategies

Small changes now can make a big difference by year-end.

3. Consider whether your business structure still fits

If your income has grown, your current entity structure might not be the most tax-efficient. For many small business owners, an S-Corporation election can create tax savings — but timing and compliance matter. Evaluating this mid-year gives you time to act before key deadlines.

The Biggest Missed Opportunity: Clean Books

Here's the reality: most tax-return issues don't start with taxes — they start with bookkeeping. If any of these sound familiar, it may be time to get help:

  • Your books are always "a few months behind"
  • You're unsure if your numbers are accurate
  • Tax season feels like a scramble every year
  • You're making decisions without clear financials

This is where consistent, professional bookkeeping changes everything.
