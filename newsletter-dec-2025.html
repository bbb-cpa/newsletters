Maximize your 2025 SALT deduction while the $40,000 cap is wide open

The new $40,000 SALT cap is in effect for 2025 through 2029 only (it drops back to $10,000 in 2030). That makes 2025 the perfect year to "bunch" as much state and local tax as you can:

  • Pay any remaining 2025 Alabama estimated income taxes (or your full balance due) in December instead of waiting until April
  • If you have a big 2025 state income tax balance, consider paying it with your December estimated payment
  • Pay the second installment of your 2025 Baldwin County property tax bill by December 31 if you're on the two-payment plan
  • Bunch other deductible expenses — charity, medical, etc. — anything to push you over the standard deduction
  • Buy equipment before New Year's — 60% bonus depreciation is still alive for 2025 purchases

Local Alert

Orange Beach & Gulf Shores owners, take note

The city tightened lodging-tax filing rules this fall. If you collected more than $2,500/month in lodging tax during 2025 (most two-bedroom units and up), you're now required to file and pay monthly instead of quarterly starting January 2026.

Airbnb and Vrbo handle the collection, but you still need to confirm the split (10% city + 4% state + 2% county) lands correctly in your MAT account.

Quick fix: log in this week, run one test filing, and you're set for the whole year.
