2026 Tax Season Is Underway: Key Dates to Know

The IRS officially began accepting 2025 tax returns on January 26, 2026. Several small-business-friendly provisions — such as the 20% Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction and expanded Section 179 expensing — are now permanent, providing greater certainty for long-term planning.

Tax Tips for Gulf Shores Small Businesses & Investors

Our local economy is driven by tourism, hospitality, real estate, and seasonal operations — factors that create unique tax considerations worth early attention.

Maximize your deductions

Carefully track deductible expenses such as home office costs, vehicle mileage, repairs, supplies, and business travel. Enhanced expensing rules and available credits may offer additional savings depending on your structure and activity.

Understand the QBI deduction

Owners of pass-through entities — S corporations, LLCs, and sole proprietorships — may be eligible to deduct up to 20% of qualified business income. Because this provision is now permanent, proper planning and documentation are essential.

Avoid common filing mistakes

The most common causes of IRS delays include missing income documents, incorrect filing status, math errors, and worker misclassification (1099 vs. W-2). Filing electronically and waiting until all documents are received can significantly reduce issues.

Stay compliant with local tax obligations

Businesses in Gulf Shores — particularly in lodging, retail, and hospitality — should regularly review sales tax and lodging tax filings to ensure accurate, timely remittance.

Bookkeeping Best Practices for a Strong 2026

  • Separate business and personal finances — dedicated accounts improve clarity, support deductions, and protect you in an audit
  • Reconcile accounts monthly — catch errors and missing transactions before they compound
  • Embrace digital recordkeeping — cloud tools make receipts, invoices, and payments easier to store and retrieve
  • Review financials quarterly — P&L and cash flow reviews reveal trends and guide proactive planning
Pro tip: Businesses with more than 30–40 transactions per month often benefit significantly from cloud-based accounting software, which reduces errors and saves time.
